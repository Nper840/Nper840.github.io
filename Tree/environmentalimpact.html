The Environmental Impact of Trees

Trees play a vital role in clearing away the world's waste C02 and supplying the world with fresh oxygen that every living organism needs to survive. Not only do trees provide homes, food and oxygen in tropical rainforests and jungles, they also exist here in modern societies. Trees that exist in our modern cities and suburbs provide these relatively new bustling, technologically advanced with oxygen, environments for animals, and natural shade and beauty. This is especially impostant bor CBHS because we are neighbors with Deans Bush, which is one of the only remaining preserved areas of New Zealands original bush. These areas must be preserved. But, as time goes on, more and more of these preservatories are getting cut down and removed to make space for houses and other commercial buildings. This is negatively inpacting our societies greatky. The lack of these trees and greenery brings up the level of carbon dioxide in our air because there are no trees to convert it into oxygen. The lack of physical greenery has also proven to worsen the mood of us humans, leading to conditions like depression and anxiety because of the lack of trees and natural wildlife.
